A living archive gets calmer once the work stops arriving as separate platform selves. Writing, sound, moving image,
and private notes can sit beside one another without pretending they were made for the same interface.

What changes here is not only storage. It is sequence. A track sketch, a short video, and a written fragment can now
read as neighboring signals in the same chronology instead of scattered proofs.
